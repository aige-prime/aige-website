ABSTRACT:

The requirements for the actual usage of artificial intelligence hinge on increased constraints as the needs grow increasingly ambitious and the surge in electricity use prove costly. Based on current research, this translates into about a 30% increase (over time) for electricity use alone and in the coming years is projected to increase.

A simple query ingested into an AI model now requires ten times as much electricity as a normal Google Search.

This raises a huge concern for sustainability as fossil fuels run at a substantial cost. The alternative, among other renewable clean energy sources, is nuclear.

With big business forging full steam ahead, the byproducts of such radical change are current hits to employment that resulnut in massive layoffs.With these underlying drivers operating at the detriment of tech employees, those who've self-invested with the hope of achieving job security turn to upskilling in order to stayahead of the pack. Others pivot by pursuing certifications offered by NVIDIA, IBM, DataCamp, and Harvard Extension School. In the current marketplace almost solely driven by artificial intelligence and machine learning models, an intersting dynamic is upscaling the frontier of our digitial world and what awaits in this new and uncharted territory requires regulation that we just might have the language to describe. Deep learning seeds these models from the most granular through to the higher-level abstractions on the subject. But what exactly is deep learning? The following notes from a recently observed crash course on the subject left me with key takeaways that help build a general framework to describe what deep learning is at its root.

Key Points & Concepts:
  • Transformer Language Model
  • DeepMind, Release Alpha Code in 2022
  • Deep Learning (Universal Association)
  • Activation Functions
  • Forward/Backward Propagation
  • Neural Networks
  • Weights & Biases that need to be adjusted as the model becomes more accurate
  • Dense Activations across the neural networks with randomly initialized weights that turn off and increase or decrease in magnitude as the data/neurons propagate across the neural network.
  • Training processes, this concept is associated with non-linear activation functions.
  • Non-Linearity, which means the activation function should not be a straight-line.
  • Optimizers help determine the direction and/or gauge efficiency with the goal being to minimize error.
  • Momentum & Pace
  • Iterations
  • Classifications
  • Patterns

      • 3 Types: Fully-Connected Feed Forward, Recurrent Neural Network (RNN), and Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)
        Feedback Loops (related to RNN)
      • Short-term memory for sequential data which ultimately remembering the most recent data points and references in bursts.

        Web-Scrapers
      • E.g. - Beautiful Soup
      • Image Classification
      • Audio
      • Pooling
      • 2D Array of Neutrons (Inputs)

    • Computational Resource Requirements
    • Sequence Order
    • Parameter Sharing
  • Data Augmentation, which is a good way to increase the dataset artificially.
  • Epoch - Passing the entire dataset through the model once.
