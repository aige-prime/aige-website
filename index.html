STUDY TOOLS & STRATEGIES:

Please note, the tools and strategies discussed below are paths I've personally used & are consistent with the methods others determined were catalysts for proven success for passing the CompTIA Security+ exam. Also, the SYO-501 will expire on July 31st, 2021 and will be replaced by the SYO-601 exam. Using Professor Messer's free videos is a great starting point and you'll find you maybe able to skip chapters or topics with other reading materials depending on your familiarity or experience. Keep in mind, I would suggest the chapter readings be mandaotry as the material is dense; the videos alone are supplemental to reading.

From here, the core of my studying came from Darrill Gibson's Study Guide for the SYO-501; I highly recommend you purchase this book or SYO-601 equivalent.Most of my peers who passed the exam also recommended his courses. Taking it myself it's truly paid off. His website https://www.getreadygetahead.com has audio files for download, practice exams, & flashcards that I used while driving to work in the mornings. PLEASE NOTE: You will have to buy the course to get access but it's really worth it! You get (60) days with the premium subscription. There's an app as well CompTIA® Security+ SY0-501 Prep which proved beneficial when I didn't have access to my laptop or hard copies resources.

"One of the most important strategies was making sure to take as many practice Performance-Based Questions (PBQ's) and practice exams prior to the exam. There's a flow state that you should aim to reach when taking the exam. This will help you gauge where you are especially if you're hitting 90% consistently on the practice exams. It took me (2) days to get out of this flow state as the material is dense. I actually felt like I could start studying for another exam like Network+ or CySA+ but it's also extremely important to pace yourself and get enough sleep."

"The problem sets in Darrill Gibson course are perfect. One thing I would also recommend is not giving up - I originally scored a 714 on my first attempt (so close...) but the second time around, I finally passed with a 774; +(60pts)! Stay postive and stay locked in! The lessons learned from that first attempt was that I didn't focus on practicing how to actually work through the PBQ's and when it came time to take the exam...I completely ran out of time. I found myself trying to figure out the mechanics of how to solve them which caused me to leave many fields blank.

"Using the printout provided by the testing center following the exam, I was able to find my trouble areas and hone in on understanding why to choose one answer over the other when faced with simliar answer choices. Sometimes you find there may be two correct answers but you have to choose the "BEST" or "LEAST" of the multiple choice options; those keywords make a huge difference. You'll be able to narrow the multiple choice down by deepening your understanding of the CompTIA® Security+ Objectives. Knowing why other answer are wrong brings things full circle and ultimately gives you extra time for more challenging problems."

"Lastly, when scheduling your exam - you might want to consider choosing a day where you wouldn't expect much traffic as there may be other students taking other exams at the same time. For example, going in on a Tuesday worked best for me as opposed to choosing Saturday. There was a noticable difference in my experience; truly night and day. The testing center should also have ear plugs handy so if you need them to block any distractions I recommend it. There's nothing worse than having your flow state broken because you hear the door open & close or notice other students struggling to get through some of thier questions."

"Best of luck and feel free to contact me for any additional help! You can reach out to me directly via email in the "Contact Me" portion of my site." - Abraham Ige
