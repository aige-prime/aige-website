In reaching this milestone, I wanted to share my journey to obtain the Security+ certification with learners who are aiming to deepen their understanding of IT from a cybersecurity perspective. When preparing for the CompTIA® Security+ exam, the first thing I did was create a plan for studying; the rest tends to fall into place once you have a well thought-out strategy.

Here's quick outline of what I did to prepare:

A). Create a Gameplan for studying. ( ***insert Pomodoro Technique here)

B). Print off the CompTIA® Security+ Objectives found on the CompTIA® official site. You'll use the objectives print out to keep track of your progress, make notes, and fill in any knowledge gaps.

C). Grab some WD-40...I mean coffee and purchase one of the many Study Guide(s)/book(s) on the Security+ Exam (preferably from Darrill Gibson).

D). Unplug - Stay focused on getting into the flow state before an exam. You will need time to rest in between exam attempts or moving to a more specialized topic. Trust the process and you'll do fine!


STUDY TOOLS & STRATEGIES:

Please note, the tools and strategies discussed below are paths I've personally used that are consistent with methods others determined led to successfully passing the exam. Also, the SY0-601 will expire on July 31st, 2024 and will be replaced by the SY0-701 exam.

 Using Professor Messer's free videos are a great starting point and you'll find you may be able to skip chapters depending on your familiarity with the objective. I would suggest the chapter readings be mandatory as the material is dense and the videos alone are supplemental to reading.

From here, the core of my studying came from Darrill Gibson's Security+ Study Guide for the Security+; I highly recommend you purchase this book or the SY0-601 equivalent. Many of my peers who've passed the exam also recommend his courses. Taking it myself, I would definitely say Darrill Gibson's course was the difference maker for me. His website https://getcertifiedgetahead.com/ provides a wealth of information. There were audio files for download, practice exams, & flashcards which were used while driving to work in the mornings.

PLEASE NOTE: You will have to buy the course to get access but it's really worth it! You get (60) days with the premium subscription. The app (CompTIA® Security+ SY0-501 Prep) proved beneficial when I didn't have access to my laptop or any hard copy resources.

"One of the most important strategies was making sure to take as many practice Performance-Based Questions (PBQs) and practice exams prior to the exam. There's a flow state that you should aim to reach when taking the exam. This will help you gauge where you are especially if you're hitting 90% consistently on the practice exams which is the goal you'll want to reach right before your scheduled exam. It took me (2) days to get out of this flow state after taking the exam (I was locked in & the material is dense). I actually felt like I could start studying for another exam like Network+ or CySA+ immediately after but keep in mind, it's extremely important to pace yourself and get enough sleep."

"Using the printout provided by the testing center following the exam, I was able to find my trouble areas and hone in on understanding why to choose one answer over the other when faced with simliar answer choices. Sometimes you find there may be two correct answers but you have to choose the "BEST" or "LEAST" of the multiple choice options; those keywords make a huge difference. You'll be able to narrow the multiple choice down by deepening your understanding of the CompTIA® Security+ Objectives. Knowing why other answers are wrong brings things full circle and ultimately gives you extra time for more challenging problems."

"Lastly, when scheduling your exam - you might want to consider choosing a day where you wouldn't expect much traffic as there may be other students taking other exams at the same time. For example, going in on a Tuesday worked best for me as opposed to choosing Saturday. There was a noticable difference in my experience; truly night and day. The testing center should also have ear plugs handy so if you need them to block any distractions I recommend it. There's nothing worse than having your flow state broken because you keep ng the door open & close or you lose focus noticing the small detail of another student having a difficult time as well getting through some of the questions."

"Best of luck and feel free to contact me for any additional help! You can reach out to me directly via email in the "Contact Me" portion of my site." - Abraham

CompTIA® Network+ & CySA+

A CompTIA Network+ certification focuses on foundational networking knowledge, while a CompTIA CySA+ certification is specifically geared towards cybersecurity analysts, validating their ability to detect, analyze, and respond to cyber threats through security analytics, intrusion detection, and incident response within a network environment; essentially, Network+ provides the network understanding needed to effectively utilize the threat detection and response skills covered in CySA+.

2024

Master's, CompTIA® PenTest+ & CASP+

A CompTIA PenTest+ certification focuses on intermediate-level penetration testing skills, signifying proficiency in identifying and exploiting vulnerabilities within systems, while a CompTIA CASP+ (Advanced Security Practitioner) certification denotes a higher level of cybersecurity expertise, indicating mastery of advanced security concepts and the ability to design and implement comprehensive security strategies across an organization.
