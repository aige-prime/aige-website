SOYINKAFESTIVAL

A Revival of Art & Spirit

"Exactly a hundred years ago, in 1912, American pioneer and producer William Fox (the founder of 20th Century Fox) raised the Audubon Ballroom (now The Malcolm X & Dr. Betty Shabazz Memorial and Educational Center). This festival will be held there on what has been a sacred cultural home for so many communities for an entire century: In the 1930s German Jewish immigrants found a home here and worshipped here until the 1980s. African Americans danced, organized, and were radicalized here throughout the middle of the 20th century. Latina/o Americans enjoyed the cinema of their traditions well into the 1970s. But there is one defining event that took place here on these hallowed grounds, which remains very important to our decision to partner with the Shabazz Center to host this SOYINKAFESTIVAL, and that is the 1965 assassination of El-Hajj Malik El-Shabazz, Malcolm X, the exact same year that Soyinka's play The Road was published. I think it is fitting and timely, not happenstance, that these two liberating legacies finally have the opportunity to speak to us in, this, our ode to the past, present, and a more public future."

Christophé Abiel, FestivalDirector

FESTIVAL UPDATES:

The first black African to win the Nobel Prize in Literature, Wole Soyinka, has given Last Heroics expressed permission to produce the New York premiere of his 1965 drama, The Road, as the centerpiece of its three day SOYINKAFESTIVAL.

The first New York production of his work after, a 25 year absence, since the 1987 Broadway premiere of his Death and the King's Horseman, Last Heroics' will helm Soyinka's gripping drama at the site of Malcolm X's 1965 assassination.
